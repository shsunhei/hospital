Side effects: Officially opened in 1984, this acute general hospital serves the Eastern New Territories region. It is the location of the Sir Yue Kong Pao Centre for Cancer and the Lady Pao Children's Cancer Centre, two state-of-the art facilities for cancer treatment, research and education, as well as the Jockey Club Trauma & Emergency Centre, which provides integrated emergency care to patients suffering from traumatic injuries and critical illness, and its outpatient services are supported by the Li Ka Shing Specialist Clinics. In addition, it is the teaching hospital of the Medical Faculty of the Chinese University of Hong Kong, and a midwifery school is attached to it.

Close

Side effects: The Hospital has been purpose-built to provide assessment, extended-care and integrated rehabilitation services to elderly, chronically-ill and acute psychiatric patients referred to it by other hospitals in the area or from the central infirmary waiting list. It is also one of three designated spinal cord injury (SCI) rehabilitation centres in Hong Kong. The Hospital aims to ensure that its patients receive a continuum of quality patient-centred and holistic care, and to contribute to improving the health of the surrounding community. To these ends, it collaborates with other health service providers, carers and community organisations to initiate joint programmes to promote health and long-term care.

Side effects: Shatin Hospital is a rehabilitation hospital, which commenced service in 1991, with emphasis on medicine and geriatrics, palliative care, surgery, adult psychiatry and psycho-geriatrics. The hospital is equipped with a Sleep Assessment Unit. It also provides community outreach services in psychiatry.
